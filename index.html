The Rise of Social Media

History

Social Media usage has grown significantly over the past 2 decades. As of writing, almost every 3 out of 5 people in the world (approximately 4.88 billion1) uses social media in some form. Today, the average user spends about 2 and a half hours on social media per day.2 Here is a brief history of how social media was developed over time:

1792 - 1996: Technological Evolution over time

Several technological innovations led to the creation of social media, particularly the following:
    1792: The telegraph was invented.3
    May 24th, 1844: First long-range electronic message is sent from Washington to Baltimore.4
    August 6th, 1991: The creation of the internet.5

1997: First Social Media Site

The first true social networking site launched publicly was called Six Degrees. It was created by Andrew Weinreich, and included features such as personal profiles, friends lists, and private messaging. However, the site failed to gain enough revenue to continue operating, and closed in 2001 around the dot-com bubble burst.6

2001-2016: Creation of Major Social Media Platforms

The Early 2000's saw the rise of many social media platforms, some of which are still industry leaders today:
    2002: LinkedIn and Friendster are launched
    2003: MySpace is launched
    2004: Facebook is launched
    2005: Youtube and Reddit are launched
    2006: Twitter (now X) is launched
    2008: Spotify is launched
    2010: Instagram and Pinterest are launched
    2011: Snapchat is launched
    2012: Facebook reaches 1 billion active users, and acquires Instagram for $1 billion CAD
    2015: Friendster shuts down
    2016: Tiktok is founded
    2018: Tiktok merges with Musical.ly and becomes popular with North American teens and young adults.

2017 - 2023: Social Media continues to Rise

Today, social media is at its highest usage rate among the global population and is projected to continue to increase. The content we consume and communication methods are continuing to evolve, with new technological developments such as filters, short-form content and AI.
